# IAU Resolution B5 — Definition of a Planet (2006)

On 24 August 2006, at its General Assembly in Prague, the International
Astronomical Union (IAU) adopted Resolution B5 defining the term "planet."
Under the new definition, a planet must (a) orbit the Sun, (b) be massive
enough to be rounded by its own gravity, and (c) have "cleared the neighbourhood"
around its orbit.

Pluto fails the third criterion and was therefore reclassified as a "dwarf planet."
Prior to this resolution, Pluto had been counted as the ninth planet of the
Solar System since its discovery in 1930.
